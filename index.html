I've been into computers for as long as I can remember.

I was always the kid messing around with things just to see how they worked — or how they could break. My first real exposure to cybersecurity was when I was about nine, figuring out how to exploit a loophole in Club Penguin's coin system. What started as a simple attempt to get more in-game currency ended up planting a seed of ingenuity and curiosity about how systems can be understood, tested, and improved.

I studied Science at Dawson College and later earned my Bachelor's in Computer Science from Concordia. Over time, I realized I'm most drawn to the side of tech that involves problem-solving and thinking a bit differently — so cybersecurity ended up being a natural fit.

Outside of that, I like to stay active, working out and playing basketball regularly. I also read a lot of fantasy and sci-fi, and I'm into amateur astronomy.
